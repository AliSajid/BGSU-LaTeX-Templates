\chapter{\texorpdfstring{INTERVIEW GUIDE}{}}

After basic introductions, the interviewer will go over the informed consent form (which will have been emailed or mailed previously to the participant) with the participant. And ask for the verbal consent.
 
After the verbal consent and with the participant’s permission, the interviewer will turn on the recording option in zoom or turn on an electronic recording device.
 
I am going to ask a series of questions regarding your life, relationships, and how you have maintained your relationships over the time as migrant in the U.S. Thank you for agreeing to talk with me about your life and relationships. We are going to begin with demographic questions.

\section{Warm-up Questions}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Can you tell me a little bit about your life now? What do you do for fun/hobbies/enjoyment? 
    \item Let’s talk a little about your childhood. Tell me about your friendships when you were a child. 
    \item How about your family relationships when you were growing up? Tell me about your parents/caregivers, siblings…Are they any other family members who were significant when you were growing up? 
    \item How did those relationships change throughout adolescence? 
    \item Let’s talk about your romantic relationships. Did you date in adolescence? What did that look like? 
    \item What does being Pakistani mean to you? How would you describe your cultural background?
    \item How connected do you feel to both Pakistani and American cultures? How has this connection changed over time?
\end{itemize}

\section{Interview with First-Generation Pakistani Immigrant Parents}

\subsection{Episode 1: Arrival and Settlement (Identity Development)}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Can you tell me about your background and how you and/or your family your children came to the United States?
    \item Can you describe your initial experiences upon arriving in the United States?
    \item What were your primary motivations for immigrating to the U.S.?
    \item How did you and your family adapt to the new environment during the early years?
    \item What were some of the biggest challenges you faced as a new immigrant?
    \item What was your sense of identity before moving to the U.S.? How did that change after you arrived?
    \item How did you balance maintaining your Pakistani cultural practices while integrating into American society for your children?
    \item Can you share a story from your early days in the U.S. that significantly shaped your sense of identity?
    \item Were there any specific cultural practices or values that you found particularly challenging to maintain?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Episode 2: Identity Negotiation}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Have you ever felt either pressure to change or hide aspects of your identity like religion, food, attires? How did you navigate those situations?
    \item How do you negotiate your identity when interacting with others who may not understand your cultural background?
    \begin{itemize}
        \item At your child’s school,
        \item At your work place
    \end{itemize}
    \item Can you describe a time when you felt conflicted between your Pakistani values and American cultural expectations?
    \item How do you navigate the differences between the expectations of your Pakistani family and values from back Is it challenging to guide and navigate your child's experiences at home in the U.S.?
    \item How integral is your religion a part of Pakistani culture for you?
    \item How do you define your association with the religion?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Episode 3: Parenting and Identity Formation}

\begin{itemize}
    \item What were your hopes and expectations for your children’s upbringing in the U.S.?
    \item How do you think your children’s experiences of identity are similar to or different from your own?
    \item How did you approach teaching your children about Pakistani culture and traditions?
    \item What role did storytelling play in conveying your cultural heritage to your children?
    \item Have you observed any conflicts or alignments between your cultural values and those of your children?
    \item What stories or lessons have you shared with your children about your experiences and identity?
    \item How do you feel about the ways your children see their Pakistani and American identities?
    \item How did you handle the differences between your cultural expectations and the societal norms in the U.S.?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Episode 4: Reflections on Identity and Legacy}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Looking back, how do you feel about your journey and the identity you have developed as an immigrant?
    \item What aspects of your cultural identity do you think have been most successfully preserved or transformed?
    \item How do you envision the future of your cultural legacy within your family?
\end{itemize}

\section{Part 2: Interview with Second-Generation Pakistani-American adult Children}

\subsection{Episode 1: Growing Up Between Cultures}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Can you describe your experiences growing up in a Pakistani-American household?
    \item What aspects of Pakistani culture did you experience frequently, and which ones were less visible in your daily life?
    \item How did you perceive the differences between your family’s culture and the mainstream American culture?
    \item Were there any specific cultural practices or traditions that you found particularly meaningful or challenging?
    \item Growing up, how did you see yourself in relation to both your Pakistani heritage and American culture?
    \item Were there moments or experiences that made you more aware of your cultural identity? Can you describe one?
    \item How has your understanding of being Pakistani-American changed as you've gotten older?
    \item How do you navigate the differences between the expectations of your Pakistani family and your experiences in American society?
    \item Have you ever felt caught between different cultural expectations? How did you handle it?
    \item In what ways do you feel your identity is different from or similar to that of your parents?
    \item How integral is religion a part of your Pakistani and individual identity?
\end{itemize}

\subsection{Episode 2: Negotiating Identity}

\begin{itemize}
    \item How did you navigate between the expectations of your Pakistani heritage and the influences of American culture?
    \item In what ways did your parents’ cultural background influence your own identity development?
    \item Have you faced any internal conflicts regarding your cultural identity? If so, how did you address them?
    \item How has your understanding of your cultural identity evolved as you’ve grown older?
    \item Can you describe any significant events or turning points that have impacted your views on your identity? In other words who you are today?

\end{itemize}

\subsection{Episode 3: Parent-Child relationship}

\begin{itemize}
    \item How do you think your experiences differ from those of your parents?
    \item What have you learned from your parents' stories about their experiences before and after they migrated?
    \item How do you think your parents’ experiences have shaped your own understanding of identity?
    \item Are there any family members, community, or social support systems that have played a role in supporting your identity development?
    \item How has your understanding of your cultural identity influenced your relationships with others both within and outside of the home and family?

\end{itemize}

\subsection{Episode 4: The Role of Storytelling}

\begin{itemize}
    \item What role did storytelling play in your understanding of your family’s history and cultural heritage?
    \item How have stories from your parents or extended family members impacted your sense of identity?
    \item Can you recall any specific stories or experiences that significantly shaped your perception of your cultural background?
    \item Do you feel that these stories have influenced how you present your cultural identity to others?

\end{itemize}

\subsection{Episode 5: Reflections and Future Directions}

\begin{itemize}
    \item Looking back, how do you view the interplay between your Pakistani heritage and your American upbringing?
    \item What aspects of your cultural identity do you find most important to preserve or adapt?
    \item How do you plan to convey your cultural heritage to future generations?
    \item What advice would you give to first-generation immigrants on navigating cultural identity with their children?
    \item Looking back on our conversation, are there any thoughts or feelings that stand out to you about your identity journey?

\end{itemize}
